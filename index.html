Rule #1: There's only one constant in this life, and that's Change.

Change is an inherent and unceasing aspect of life. It touches every aspect of our world, from the seasons, to personal growth, to societal evolution. And it underlies the idea that adaptability, resilience, and open-mindedness are valuable qualities in navigating the complex and ever-changing nature of life.

Rule #2: حسبنا الله ونعم الوكيل

Understand sometimes Allah may have better different plans for us, one that may be completely different from what we conjured up in our heads. Submitting to the will of Allah and leaving your affairs to Him is like lifting a heavy load off your shoulders. It is a spiritual healing touch in one’s life. “And whoever relies upon Allah, then He is sufficient for him.” (Qur’an, 65:3)

Rule #3: Nothing is True, Everything is Permitted.

To say that nothing is true is to realize that the foundations of society are fragile and that we must be the shepherds of our civilization. To say that everything is permitted is to understand that we are the architects of our actions, and that we must live with their consequences, whether glorious or tragic.

Rule #4: Everyone has their own lives. No two lives are comparable.

Each person's life journey is unique, shaped by individual experiences, choices, and circumstances. This rule emphasizes the importance of recognizing and respecting the diversity of human experiences and avoiding comparisons, as each person's path is distinct and incomparable to another's.

Rule #5: If you think certain something could happen due to 99 reasons, it may not always be true. That something, might happen due to a 100th reason.

This means that outcomes are often unpredictable and not always explained by the most apparent factors. It emphasizes the complexity of life and the idea that unexpected factors can influence events, reminding us to remain open to possibilities beyond our expectations and assumptions.

Rule #6: Sometimes, you are wrong. Accept it, it's Okay.

This means that making mistakes is a natural part of life and personal growth. It emphasizes the importance of humility, self-awareness, and learning from errors. Accepting that we can be wrong helps us grow, improves our decision-making, and fosters better relationships with others.

Rule #7: No matter how independent you want to be, sometimes you need help. Ask for it.

This rule underscores the importance of recognizing our own limitations and the value of seeking assistance when necessary. It encourages humility and pragmatism, acknowledging that seeking help is not a sign of weakness but a practical approach to overcoming challenges and achieving goals more effectively.

Rule #8: Learn to forgive people.

This rule emphasizes the importance of forgiveness in personal growth and relationships. Forgiving others allows us to let go of negative emotions, resentment, and grudges. It promotes healing and emotional well-being, enabling us to move forward without being burdened by past grievances. Forgiveness also fosters empathy and understanding, contributing to healthier and more harmonious interactions with others.

Rule #9: Be thankful for what you have. Always.

Say ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّٰهِ , always. It encourages gratitude as a fundamental attitude towards life. It emphasizes appreciating and acknowledging the blessings, opportunities, and positive aspects of one's life, regardless of circumstances. Cultivating a mindset of thankfulness fosters contentment, resilience, and a positive outlook. It helps individuals focus on what they have rather than what they lack, promoting emotional well-being and satisfaction with life.

Rule #10: Seek Knowledge Continuously. Learning is a lifelong journey.

This rule underscores the importance of ongoing education and personal development. It encourages a mindset of curiosity, exploration, and intellectual growth throughout one's life. Embracing learning as a lifelong journey allows us to adapt to change, stay relevant in a dynamic world, and broaden their perspectives. It promotes self-improvement, innovation, and a deeper understanding of oneself and the world.

Rule #11: If this World doesn't have answers for you, do Istikhara. You'll always find guidance.

Seek guidance from Allah when faced with important decisions or uncertainties. It emphasizes relying on spiritual guidance when worldly knowledge or advice is insufficient. Istikhara provides clarity and direction, helping individuals make decisions that align with God's will and bring peace of mind. It encourages trust in divine wisdom and guidance in navigating life's challenges and choices. But, always remember it takes time, have patience.
